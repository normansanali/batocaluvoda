Professional Life

Caption: Annika Sorenstam with her husband(source: Instagram)

Annika Sorenstam is a former Swedish Professional Golf Player. She rose to the limelight after winning the Women’s British Open and World Golf Hall of Fame in 2003.

Her impressive amateur career includes a victory in the St Rule Trophy at St Andrews and a runner-up position in the Swedish national mother/daughter championship.

She competed in the Espirito Santo Trophy in 1990 and 1992 as a member of the Swedish National Team from 1987 to 1992, winning the solo competition in 1992.

Sorenstam worked as a personal assistant at the Swedish PGA and competed on the Swedish Golf Tour while waiting to enter college in Sweden, winning three events in 1990/1991.
