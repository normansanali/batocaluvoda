By profession, Clay Aiken is a talented singer. Clay Aiken could be able to gain fame after participating in the second season of the reality show, American Idol.  Also, he is prominently known for his amazing projects such as  Merry Christmas with Love, A Thousand Different Ways, All is Well, and On My Way Here. 

Early Life

The birthname of Clay Aiken is Clayton Holmes Grisson. He was born on November 30, 1978. His birthplace is in Raleigh, North Carolina. Presently, he is 45 years old. He has a birth sign of Sagittarius. Similarly, he belongs to the American nationality. But, his ethnicity is not known.

Education

Initially, he attended Raleigh’s Leesville Road High School and Campell University for his high school studies. During his school days, he was a member of several church choirs and school choirs. He was also with the local school band, Just By Chance. His first performance was with the song, Islands in the Stream. When he was only five, he performed it in his high school dance in 1983. Later on, he studied ‘Special Education’ at the University of North Carolina at Charlotte. Though he attended William & Mary College, he left when he participated in American Idol.

Career and Professional Life

A career as a singer

In the bargain, he has extended his career as a singer, actor, politician, and also activist. As a singer, his debut album was Measure of a Man. In 2003 he then appeared in the Christmas Day Parade. Also, he produced a TV special, A Clay Aiken Christmas the next year. He then released a holiday album, Merry Christmas With Love.

Moreover, his albums are A Thousand Different Ways (2006), All Is Well (2006), On My Way Here (2008), The Very Best of Clay Aiken (2008), and Tried and True (2009). Similarly, his new single in 2011 is Bring Back My Love. The singer released his new single, Steadfast in 2012.

Awards and Nominations

Amazingly, there are so many awards and nominations for Clay Aiken. He was the winner of the American Music Award for the title of Fan’s Choice in 2003. In the same year, he received the Billboard Award for Best Selling Single of 2003-Bridge Over Troubled Water/ This Is the Night.

Consecutively, he achieved the Billboard Award for Best Selling Christmas/Christian Album- Merry Christmas with Love. Also, he won the New Music Weekly Award for Top 40 Male Artist of the Year in 2004.

In 2005, he was the winner of the American Christian Music Award for Outstanding Yule CD- Merry Christmas With Love. He then won the Robert M. Barg Memorial Achievement Award as well. Similarly, he secured the award of the National Center for Learning Disabilities Children’s Advocacy Award in 2007. Ultimately, he obtained The Family Circle Award from the Family Equality Council in 2009.

Talking about the nominations, he has been nominated for three award titles. In 2003, he was nominated for the American Music Award for Favorite Male Artist Pop/Rock, and the Teen Choice Award for Choice TV Male Reality Star (American Idol). In 2004,  he was nominated for the Billboard Music Award for Top Selling R&B/ Hip=Hop Single of the Year (The Way/Solitaire).
