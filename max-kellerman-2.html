All you need to know about Max Kellerman

Max Kellerman is a sportscaster with a $6 million net worth. Max Kellerman has hosted several ESPN series, including “SportsNation,” “Around the Horn,” “First Take,” and “This Just In With Max Kellerman.”

He also co-hosted ESPN’s radio shows “Max & Marcellus” and “Keyshawn, JWill and Max.” Kellerman has also provided commentary for the programs “Friday Night Fights,” “HBO World Championship Boxing,” and “Boxing After Dark.”

Who is Max Kellerman?

Max Kellerman was born on August 6, 1973, in the Bronx borough of New York City and grew up in Greenwich Village as the eldest of four brothers. In his adolescence, he attended PS 41 and Hunter College High School. Kellerman received his higher education at Columbia University, where he graduated in 1998 with a BA in history.

What is Max Kellerman’s career?

Kellerman began his career in sports broadcasting as a youngster, hosting a public-access television program called “Max on Boxing.” After graduating from Columbia, he worked as an analyst for ESPN’s “Friday Night Fights” boxing show.

Kellerman went on to design and host “Around the Horn,” a sports roundtable discussion show that debuted on ESPN in late 2002. Despite the program’s popularity, Kellerman left ESPN in early 2004 after failing to strike a deal to remain with the network. He then started hosting a new show on Fox Sports Net called “I, Max,” where he analyzed sports news with Michael Holly.

That program was ultimately canceled in early 2005. The next year, Kellerman began hosting shows on WEPN in New York City and joined the HBO boxing show “Boxing After Dark,” where he provided analysis with Fran Charles and Lennox Lewis.

In 2007, he was added to the “HBO World Championship Boxing” crew, which included Jim Lampley, Harold Lederman, and a rotating guest commentator.

In the spring of 2010, CNN hired Kellerman to cover sports and pop culture for the network’s numerous shows. At the end of the year, it was revealed that he would join ESPNLA as a cohost of the sports radio talk show “Max & Marcellus,” alongside Marcellus Wiley. The pair returned as cohosts of the sports humor talk show “SportsNation” in 2013, and were soon joined by Michelle Beadle in 2014. Kellerman left the show in 2016 to become an analyst on Molly Qerim’s sports talk show “First Take,” succeeding Skip Bayless. After quitting the show in 2021, he started anchoring “This Just In with Max Kellerman,” a sports talk show.
