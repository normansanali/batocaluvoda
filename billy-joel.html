Billy Joel – Professional Life and Career

In 1963, he founded his first band, ‘The Echoes,’ which later became known as ‘The Lost Souls.’ In 1967, he left ‘The Lost Souls to form a new band, ‘The Hassles.’ ‘The Hassles,’ the band’s debut album, came out in 2009, followed by ‘Hour of the Wolf,’ the following year. He quit the band in 1969, along with drummer Joe Small, and created ‘Attila,’ a heavy metal pair with him. In July 1970, they released an album through Epic Records.

He secured a solo recording deal with ‘Family Productions in 1971, and his debut solo album, ‘Cold Spring Harbor,’ was released in 1972. ‘WMMR-FM,’ a Philadelphia radio station, began broadcasting his live concert recording ‘Captain Jack,’ which quickly became a chartbuster on the East Coast. The executives of ‘Columbia Records gave him a recording contract after hearing the song.
