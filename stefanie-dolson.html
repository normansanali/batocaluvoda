Stefanie Dolson is an American professional basketball player who has represented Washington Mystics (2014-2016) and Chicago Sky (2017-Present) in the WNBA (Women’s National Basketball Association). She was named in the WNBA ‘All-Star Team’ in both 2015 and 2017. During the WNBA off-season, she has represented clubs around the world such as WBC Spartak Moscow Region (2014–2015) in Russia, Mersin Büyükşehir Belediyesi (2015–2016) in Turkey, Sichuan Whale (2016) in China, Dike Napoli (2017–2018) in Italy, and Henan Phoenix (2018–Present) in China. At the international level, Stefanie was an integral member of the United States squad that won the 2010 FIBA (Fédération Internationale de Basketball) Americas Under-18 Championship For Women with a 5-0 record. The triumph helped the United States qualify directly for the 2011 FIBA Under-19 Women’s World Championship. She was named in that squad as well which went on to win the ‘Gold’ medal at the tournament with an 8-1 record.

    Stefanie Dolson Facts

  • During her high school career, Stefanie led Minisink Valley High School to the final of the New York State Public High School Athletic Association Section 9 Championship for 4 years in succession.
  • In her senior year of high school, in 2010, she was named a McDonald’s High School All-American and had competed in the McDonald’s All-American High School Game.
  • While playing for the University of Connecticut (2010–2014), she won the NCAA (National Collegiate Athletic Association) Championship in successive years, in 2013 and 2014. In her senior year (2014), she was named both the ‘AAC (American Athletic Conference) Defensive Player of the Year’ and the ‘WBCA (Women’s Basketball Coaches Association) Defensive Player of the Year’.
  • Owing to her stellar performances at the collegiate level, she was picked 6th overall in the 1st round of the 2014 WNBA draft by the Washington Mystics.
  • She came out as bisexual in 2016 during an interview with ESPN Magazine where she was speaking in support of the LGBTQ community.
  • In April 2020, her entire family had contracted the dreaded coronavirus infection and her mother even had to be hospitalized for about 4 days.
